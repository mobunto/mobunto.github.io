Biography

Hello there! My name is Oybek, and I am a Central Asian immigrant who moved to the United States in December 2007 from one of the only two double-landlocked countries called Uzbekistan.

Ever since I was a kid, I loved tinkering around with the family computer. I would often break something within the operating system but would always find ways to fix it with the power of Google! I'd often spend my free time reading and watching videos about the latest tech (gadgets, computer hardware, tech leaks, etc.) and playing video games.

Eventually, I built my first computer when I was 14 and started learning programming through Codecademy. Afterwards, I would build computers for others or help them pick out parts.

After graduating high school, I designed a 4-zoned sprinkler control system that was controlled by a Raspberry Pi with a custom GUI. Through the GUI, the user could set a schedule and view real-time weather data, which the controller used to save water on rainy days.

Currently, I work as a Controls Technical Trainer at the Amazon Center of Excellence. As a trainer, my main task is to train new and veteran technicians/engineers on the various control systems used to automate Amazon warehouses. Alongside my main task, I also travel to warehouses to provide controls support.

Before becoming a trainer, I helped launch KCVG, an Amazon Air Hub, as a Controls Technician. Before launch, I worked with stakeholders to inspect control cabinets, create a flow map of the building, and associate the Measuring Points (MPs) in the flow map to physical assets. After launch, I worked on multiple projects with operations, maintenance team, automation engineers, and the systems integrators to make improvements to the control systems and metrics on site.
